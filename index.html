I am a Provost Fellow at the Sonny Astani Department of Civil & Environmental Engineering, University of Southern California. I am in the 3rd year of my PhD program. I am advised by Prof. Erik A. Johnson.

Broadly my research interests are in uncertainty quantification (UQ) of dynamical systems. I explore surrogate modelling techniques for forward UQ tasks, such as reduced order modelling, multi-fidelity methods, and data-driven methods including scientific machine learning. I am particularly interested in techniques that are resource and data frugal, and noise tolerant. I also work on model validation, used for making inverse problems computationally efficient.

I graduated (2017) summa cum laude from the Department of Civil Engineering, Indian Institute of Science with a Masters in Civil Engineering and a Major in Structural Engineering. I was advised by Prof. Debraj Ghosh. There I worked on reliability estimation of dynamical systems using reduced-order models. I also expect to obtain a Master's in Electrical Engineering from the Ming Hsieh Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering, USC soon (Fall 2019).

Failure probability estimation of linear time varying systems by progressive refinement of reduced order models
Agnimitra Dasgupta and Debraj Ghosh
SIAM/ASA Journal on Uncertainty Quantification, Vol. 7, Issue 3, Pages 1007–1028.
journal paper

We propose a novel iterative algorithm for estimating failure probabilities of parametrically uncertain linear dynamical systems using ROMs. The key idea in this algorithm is to progressively localize the training domain of the ROM in the domain of random parameters. A propoer orthogonal decompositon based reduced order modelling approach, adapted from this paper, for linear time varying systems is also explored.

Characterization of spatial heterogeneity in material properties
Agnimitra Dasgupta, Erik A Johnson and Steven F Wojtkiewicz

We explore a computationally efficient approach to characterize material spatial heterogeneity based on noisy strain or displacement measurements. The work has important applications including material characterization and structural health monitoring. In this work, we use a probabilistic hybrid framework to identify the model class(es) that best describes the available data.

This work is partially supported by the National Science Foundation through awards CMMI 16-63667/16-62992.
