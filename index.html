I am a 3rd year Ph.D. student at the Sonny Astani Department of Civil & Environmental Engineering, University of Southern California. I am advised by Prof. Erik A. Johnson. Broadly my research interests are in uncertainty quantification (UQ) of dynamical systems. I explore surrogate modelling techniques for forward UQ tasks, such as reduced order modelling, multi-fidelity methods, and data-driven methods including scientific machine learning. I am particularly interested in techniques that are resource and data frugal, and noise tolerant. I also work on model validation, used for making inverse problems computationally efficient.

I graduated (2017) summa cum laude from the Department of Civil Engineering, Indian Institute of Science with a Masters in Civil Engineering and a Major in Structural Engineering. I was advised by Prof. Debraj Ghosh. There I worked on reliability estimation of dynamical systems using reduced-order models.

I also expect to obtain a Master's in Electrical Engineering from the Ming Hsieh Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering, USC soon (Fall 2019).

Research

I explore dialog systems, question-answering, assistive generation and broadly various natural language generation tasks. I'm interested in developing generative NLP models catering to personalization, commonsense reasoning and subjective question answering broadly connecting to conversational modeling. My previous research on NLP includes sequence labeling, sequence generation, and natural language parsers. I also worked on statistical modeling, game theory, and machine learning applications.

Generating Personalized Recipes from Historical User Preferences
Bodhisattwa P. Majumder*, Shuyang Li*, Jianmo Ni, Julian McAuley
2019 Conference on Empirical Methods in Natural Language Processing (EMNLP)
pdf | code | data

We propose a new task of personalized recipe generation to help these users: expanding a name and incomplete ingredient details into complete natural-text instructions aligned with the user's historical preferences.

When lolcats meet philosoraptors! - What's in a 'meme'?
Bodhisattwa P. Majumder, Amrith Krishna, Unni Krishnan, Anil K. Boga, Animesh Mukherjee
Arxiv , 2018
pdf | presentation

How similar are the dynamics of meme based communities to that of text based communities? We try to explain the community dynamics by categorising each day based on temporal variations in the user engagement. This work was done in a collaboration with CNeRG.
